## What to add (sparingly — 8-15 touches total)

**Similes** — compare technical concepts to everyday things:
- "like handing someone a magnifying glass and pointing at a building"
- "like a bridge with 80 bolts where 5 are missing"

**Metaphors** — make abstract things concrete:
- "a mirror as much as a microscope"
- "the git equivalent of five chefs sharing one cutting board"

**Analogies** — connect unfamiliar to familiar:
- "discovered defects the way you discover a hole in your umbrella — by getting rained on"
- "like a receptionist who only says 'ask the person down the hall'"

**Punch lines** — reward the reader at key moments:
- "Sometimes the sequel is shorter than the blooper reel"
- "In open source, it's perfectly fine to arrive second with the right answer"

**Witty observations** — gentle, never sarcastic:
- "missing its own name" (for a command without a name: field)
- "a quiet acknowledgment that the diagnosis came from somewhere worth naming"

**Light jokes** — only where the narrative earns one:
- After a failure: a self-deprecating quip
- After an ironic outcome: note the irony gently

## Where to place them

1. **Title** — creative, not generic ("When the Linter Met Its Match" not "NLPM Audit Report")
2. **Opening** — the first paragraph after disclosure sets the tone
3. **After failures** — the parallel agent mess, rejected PRs, duplicate closures
4. **Transition sentences** — between major sections
5. **The fairness note** — warmth, not formality
6. **Closing paragraph** — the most memorable line in the piece goes here

## Rules

- SOFT TONE — never presumptuous, never mocking the target project
- Polite — these are other people's hard work
- Do NOT add ornament to technical tables, code blocks, mermaid blocks, or data
- Do NOT change any facts, numbers, links, or evidence
- Do NOT add more than 15 touches — restraint is elegance
- Each touch must serve the narrative, not just decorate

## Process

1. Read the full article
2. Identify the 10-15 strongest spots for literary touches
3. Apply each using the Edit tool — add phrases, swap adjectives, insert one-liners
4. Do NOT rewrite paragraphs — this is seasoning, not cooking
